What is a Soapberry

Soapberry's leathery skin is rich in saponin, a natural surfactant that makes it the rawest form of soap. Since ancient times, these berries have been valued for its anti-bacterial, medicinal, and dermatological benefits in sub-tropical Asia. Before chemical cleaners were introduced, the soapberry was the preferred method of cleaning.

When rubbed in your hands, the fruit forms a rich, creamy lather that reconnects you with nature

Shampoo / Body Gel
