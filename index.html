What is a Soapberry

Soapberry's leathery skin is rich in saponin, a natural surfactant that makes it the rawest form of soap. Since ancient times, these berries have been valued for its anti-bacterial, medicinal, and dermatological benefits in sub-tropical Asia. Before chemical cleaners were introduced, the soapberry was the preferred method of cleaning.

Shampoo / Body Wash

Once a year, our family farmers forage wild soapberries from the tropical forests of southern Taiwan. Through an exclusive and eco-friendly extraction method for extracting the active interface saponin from soapberries, we obtain the highest quality concentrate of their natural cleaning power to produce a variety of gentle, natural, non-toxic soapberry shampoo, shower gel, and eventually a whole suite of natural body care treasures.
